"We cannot hope to create a sustainable culture with any but sustainable souls"-by Derrick Jensen

Waste-are classified as wasted material for which no further use is made by the owner or recipient as stated by the United Nation Framework for
Development of Environmental statistics.Waste is an emerging issue globally with the increasing global human population, technological
advancement in various field of science, art and commerce and the changing lifestyle.There has been a massive increase in the waste production
as well as an increase in the waste management issues associated with it.

Globally, 2.01 billion tonnes of soiid waste are generated annually and it is projected to increase to 3.4 billion tonnes by 2050.Moreover,at least 33%
of this waste are not managed in a environmentally safe manner. In accordance, waste management is a concern at the national level as well especially
for the developing nations which lacks finance and infrastructures to manage the wastes. Similar is the case with Bhutan, a developing nation located
in the Eastern Himalaya bordered by two gigantic nations China in the north and India in the south. According to the nation's first waste inventory
report Bhutan, generates more than 170 metric tons of waste daily.Thus, managing these massive waste produced daily is a pressing issue growing
continuously over the decades. Waste is also associated to cause impact on the environment (that includes air, water and soil) as well as affects the
human health due to most of it being hazardous, sensitive and toxic in nature.


IMPACTS OF WASTE ON ENVIRONMENT AND HUMAN HEALTH

In order to manage the waste efficiently globally as well as at national and grass root level various organizations, institution, non-governmental agencies
and individual have signed numerous conventions, framed various policies, rules and regulation.However, there is lack of assessment on the progress of the
initiatives once it is implemented.

Moreover, according to global waste statistics, more than 33% of the waste are dumped in open spaces and approximately more than 25.2 are disposed into
in the landfill(which are unspecified).Only 13.5% of the wastes are recycled thus, only a small portion of the waste is repurposed whereas most of it
is discarded and not properly managed despite having numerous other usable potentials.

so, to efficiently manage the growing waste management issues and impacts related to it, sustainable ways are to be adopted and implemented by sustainable
souls. A sustainable development approach should be taken into action so that minmial waste is wasted and maximum portion of it is reutilized.
